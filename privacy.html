Privacy Policy

Athanor: Alchemy Merge

Effective · 20 May 2026

Athanor: Alchemy Merge is a single-player discovery game published by Pizza Hero Gaming. This policy explains what data the app touches, why, and what you can do about it. We've kept it plain.

Short Version

We don't ask you for a name, email, or account. Your discoveries stay on your device. The only third party that receives data is Google AdMob, which shows the rewarded ads that power the in-game Hint feature.

1. Information We Collect

From you, directly: nothing. Athanor has no sign-in, no profile, no online account, and no chat. We collect no name, email address, phone number, or contact list.

On your device (local only): the list of elements you have discovered, your save slots, your in-game settings (sound, layout, filters), and tutorial progress. This data is stored in your device's local storage and is never transmitted to us or anyone else. Uninstalling the app deletes it.

Automatically, via Google AdMob: when you choose to watch a rewarded ad to receive a Hint, the Google Mobile Ads SDK that powers those ads may collect:

  • Your device's Advertising ID (a resettable identifier you control in your Android settings)
  • Approximate location, derived from your IP address (country / region level)
  • Device information: model, operating system version, language, screen size
  • Ad interaction events: that an ad was shown and whether it completed

This data is processed by Google for ad delivery, ad measurement, and limiting how often you see the same ad. We do not see this data directly; we only see aggregate impression counts and revenue figures in our AdMob dashboard. For Google's own description of how they handle this data, see their Advertising Policies and Privacy Policy.

2. How Your Data Is Used

  • To run the game itself — saving your discoveries on your device so you can return to them.
  • To show rewarded ads — only when you tap the Hint button and confirm "Watch ad." Ads never play automatically.
  • To measure ad delivery — aggregate counts of impressions and rewards. We do not build a profile of you.

We do not use your data for targeted advertising outside the app. We do not sell your data. We do not share it with anyone other than Google AdMob (described above).

3. Your Choices

  • Reset your Advertising ID in your Android Settings → Privacy → Ads. This severs the link between previous ad activity and your device.
  • Opt out of ad personalization in the same Settings menu. Ads still play, but they're non-personalized.
  • Don't watch ads at all — every ad in Athanor is opt-in via the Hint button. The game is fully playable without ever tapping it.
  • Delete all data by uninstalling the app, or from inside the game via Settings → Reset Progress.

4. Children's Privacy

Athanor is intended for players aged 13 and older. It is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children under 13. If you are a parent or guardian and believe your child has interacted with the app, please contact us and we will assist in resolving the matter.

5. International Users (GDPR, CCPA, etc.)

Athanor is available worldwide. If you live in the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, California, or another jurisdiction with similar laws, you have the right to access, correct, or delete data held about you, and to object to certain processing. Because we don't hold any personal data about you ourselves, requests of this nature typically need to go to Google rather than us. For Google AdMob data requests, see Google's data & privacy tools.

Where applicable, the legal basis for our use of advertising-related data via Google AdMob is your consent or our legitimate interest in funding free distribution of the app.

6. Security

Data exchanged with Google's ad servers is transmitted over standard HTTPS encryption built into the Google Mobile Ads SDK. Game state stored on your device uses standard Android local storage and is sandboxed to the app.

7. Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy as the app changes — for example, if we add new features that handle data differently. We'll change the "Effective" date above when we do, and we'll note material changes on our Discord server. Continued use of the app after a change means you accept the revised policy.
